About The Color Pink.

 

 

 

Pink is the color of happiness and is sometimes seen as lighthearted. For women who are often overworked and overburdened, an attraction to pink may speak of a desire for the more carefree days of childhood. Bright pinks, like the color red, stimulate energy and can increase the blood pressure, respiration, heartbeat, and pulse rate. They also encourage action and confidence. Pink has been used in prison holding cells to effectively reduce erratic behavior.1

The color pink resonates with our heart chakra, and is associated with love and kindness. It’s also associated with good health ('in the pink') and happiness ('tickled pink').2

Pink is feminine and romantic, affectionate and intimate, thoughtful and caring. It tones down the physical passion of red replacing it with a gentle loving energy. This color represents compassion, nurturing and love. It relates to unconditional love and understanding, and the giving and receiving of nurturing.3
